Lore of Emberhollow

What is written here is what the town admits to. The rest is in the books — if you can find them.

The Town

Emberhollow sits where the east road meets the sea, walled on its north side against something nobody names anymore. The well came first, they say; the town grew around the water like a ring around a stone.

The Watch

The Watch never sleeps — though it does, famously, take lunch. Their rounds trace the old roads nightly, and their reports of lights near the tower have been filed, stamped, and ignored for three generations.

The Sea

Captain Brammel says the sea has been generous lately. Suspiciously generous. Fishermen have lost lines to something big beyond the shallows, and nobody has yet volunteered to find out what.

The Cave

Beyond the western fields there is sometimes a cave. Old Shea saw it as a boy. Sharp-eyed travelers see it still — a mouth of stone that hums, ringed in violet, gone by morning. Those who enter come back with rune shards, small fortunes, and very little they can explain.

The Books

Pages of the town's true history are scattered across Emberhollow — the Bibliofolio. Each recovered volume sharpens the mind of its finder. Collect them all, and perhaps the lights, the cave, and the quiet mill will start to make sense.
